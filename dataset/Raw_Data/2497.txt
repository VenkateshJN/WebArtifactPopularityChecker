England elect to field against Sri Lanka in lone T20I The author has posted comments on this article AFP | May 20 , 2014 , 10.38PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 LONDON : England captain Eoin Morgan won the toss and elected to field against Sri Lanka in the lone Twenty20 international at The Oval on Tuesday . England gave Twenty20 international debuts to Hampshire opener Michael Carberry and Nottinghamshire left-arm seamer Harry Gurney , while batsman Ian Bell was playing his first match at this level since January 2011 . Former Ireland batsman Morgan was leading England in the absence of injured fast bowler Stuart Broad . Sri Lanka are the reigning World Twenty20 champions but on their way to taking the title they suffered a six-wicket defeat by England in Chittagong in March -- the last time the teams met . In that match , opener Alex Hales , selected for Tuesday 's fixture , made an unbeaten 116 -- the first Twenty20 international hundred by an England batsman . Sri Lanka came into this match on the back of a 10-wicket Twenty20 thrashing of county side Sussex on Sunday . This was the first international of Sri Lanka 's tour of England that also includes five-match one-day and two-Test series .
