SC allows telecast of World Cup matches on cable The author has posted comments on this article TNN | Feb 11 , 2015 , 11.16AM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 NEW DELHI : The Supreme Court stayed a Delhi High Court order restraining Doordarshan from sharing live feed of cricket World Cup matches with cable operators. At the outset of the hearing , a bench of Justices Ranjan Gogoi and P C Ghose said the matter deserved full hearing and stayed the HC order . Senior advocates P Chidambaram and Salman Khurshid , appearing for ESPN and Star TV which have exclusive broadcasting rights , however , pleaded that some interim arrangement must be made to ensure that the companies did not suffer revenue loss . The broadcasters pleaded for an interim arrangement. The bench posted the case for hearing on February 17 and asked ESPN and Star to come out with proposals . The court said till then , the public broadcaster will continue to share live feed with cable operators which means people can watch World Cup games through cable free of cost till an interim arrangement is put in place .
