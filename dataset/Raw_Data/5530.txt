O'Brien fined for showing dissent Wed , Feb 25 2015 Last updated on Wednesday , 25 February , 2015 , 05 : 58 PM O'Brien showed his displeasure at a " wide " call. © AFP Ireland allrounder Kevin O'Brien has been fined 30 per cent of his match fee for questioning an umpiring decision in his team 's two-wicket win against United Arab Emirates in the ICC World Cup in Brisbane on Wednesday , an ICC press release said . The incident happened in the 48th over when the burly allrounder , who contributed 50 off 25 balls in his team 's successful chase later in the day , showed his displeasure at a " wide " call . The release said : " O'Brien was found to have breached Article 2.1.3 of the ICC Code of Conduct for Players and Player Support Personnel , which relates to 'showing dissent at an umpire 's decision during an international match' . " After the conclusion of the match , O'Brien admitted the offence and accepted the sanction proposed by Ranjan Madugalle of the Emirates Elite Panel of ICC Match Referees. As such , there was no need for a formal hearing . " The charge was laid by on-field umpires Michael Gough and Nigel Llong , third umpire Kumar Dharmasena and fourth umpire Billy Bowden.
