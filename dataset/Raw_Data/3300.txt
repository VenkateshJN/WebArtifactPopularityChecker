AAP condemns ICC 's decision of denying entry of Sikhs in cricket stadium The author has posted comments on this article Yudhvir Rana , TNN | Mar 16 , 2015 , 06.50PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 AMRITSAR : Senior advocate of Supreme Court and senior Aam Aadmi Party ( AAP ) leader HS Phoolka has condemned the decision of International Cricket Council 's ( ICC ) to deny entry of Amritdhari Sikhs carrying kirpans into the stadium during a match played between India and Zimbabwe in New Zealand . Phoolka on Monday said that seven Sikh cricket fans were stopped from entering Eden Park to watch India play Zimbabwe in a match on Saturday as they were carrying kirpans . " A kirpan is a religious and pious symbol for a baptised Sikh and emotions and religious sentiments are attached with this , which is one of the five symbols of Sikhism , " said Phoolka . He said that even New Zealand government had also condemned the decision and had sympathized with the community . He said he had urged the central government to take up the matter seriously with ICC as Indians were prominently placed in ICC .
