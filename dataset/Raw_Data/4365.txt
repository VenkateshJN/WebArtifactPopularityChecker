Trent Bridge pitch dubbed 'poor ' by Boon The author has posted comments on this article Sumit Mukherjee , TNN | Jul 20 , 2014 , 10.17AM IST ICC issued a statement , saying Match Referee David Boon had rated the pitch used at Trent Bridge for first Test as " poor " . ( TOI Photo ) Page 1 of 4 LONDON : The Nottinghamshire County Cricket Club on Saturday earned the International Cricket Council 's ( ICC 's ) wrath for producing a lifeless track at the Trent Bridge for the first Test between England and India. The match ended in a tame draw last week . The world body issue a statement , saying Match Referee David Boon had rated the pitch used at Trent Bridge for first Test as " poor " . " In accordance with Clause 3 of the ICC Pitch Monitoring Process , Mr. Boon submitted his report to the ICC expressing his concerns over the quality of the pitch. This report has been forwarded to the England and Wales Cricket Board ( ECB ) , which now has 14 days to provide its response , " an ICC release said . ICC 's General Manager ( Cricket ) , Geoff Allardice , and the Chief Match Referee , Ranjan Madugalle , will wait for ECB 's response and consider all evidence , including studying video footage of the match , before reaching their decision . If the pitch is deemed to be poor , a penalty could be imposed in accordance with Clause 4 of the ICC Pitch Monitoring Process. There were no demons in the pitch on which India scored 457 and 391-9 dec , while England totaled 496 in its only innings .
