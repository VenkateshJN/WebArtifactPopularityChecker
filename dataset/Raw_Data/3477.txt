Clarke feared career was over after Adelaide Test The author has posted comments on this article PTI | Feb 13 , 2015 , 01.02PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 SYDNEY : On a comeback trail following a hamstring surgery , Australian captain Michael Clarke said he feared his career was finished after the Adelaide Test against India . The 33-year-old veteran will sit out Saturday 's World Cup opener against traditional rival England . " When scans confirmed I had torn my hamstring ( after the last day of the Adelaide Test against India ) I thought I might just have played my last game of cricket , " Clarke was quoted as saying by the Sydney Daily Telegraph . " I had suffered my fourth hamstring in six months and my back was flaring up . " I had just scored a century in memory of my little 'brother ' ( Phillip Hughes , the Test player who died after being hit by a bouncer in a domestic game in November ) in a winning Test side . " If that was to be the end , I thought , I would have no regrets. " Clarke said he had learnt to put his career worries in perspective following the death of Hughes and his father 's diagnosis of cancer in 2007 . " My attitude changed a lot. To that point I had been totally consumed by cricket. But that all turned upside down the day I learnt dad had Hodgkin 's lymphoma .
