Did Dravid find coaches ' programme obsolete ? The author has posted comments on this article Arani Basu , TNN | Apr 2 , 2015 , 09.39AM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 NEW DELHI : Talk about producing good Indian coaches and the names of Sanjay Bangar , Bharat Arun and R Sridhar come immediately to the mind . The remarkable improvement in Indian team 's performances since the induction of these three coaches in August last year , has salvaged the reputation of coaches in India . But the next rung of coaches has hit a roadblock. The Indian Cricket Board ( BCCI ) is yet to devise or revise the coaches ' programme which has stalled for nearly two years now . Despite the board 's court shenanigans being settled , the programme is yet to take off as it is learnt that the existing procedure and coaching methods have been dubbed 'obsolete' . TOI has learnt that National Cricket Academy president TC Mathew had sought Rahul Dravid 's opinion on the manual and process which the latter thought was pretty irrelevant for the present game . " Dravid said that the methods the coaches were taught is outdated. The game has moved on so much , there have to be new and innovative methods in the manual. The manual and the entire programme needed to be rejigged. The board is still formularizing the programme , " said a source close to the development .
