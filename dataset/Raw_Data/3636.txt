Game changer - Kevin O'Brien : The demolition man The author has posted comments on this article TNN | Jan 30 , 2015 , 01.45PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 The Irish have punched way above their weight in the last couple of World Cups. In 2007 they left Pakistan cricket in tatters with a shocking reverse in the West Indies and then in 2011 , they socked old foes England in the eye at Bengaluru 's Chinnaswamy Stadium . Much of the damage was done by the robust Kevin O'Brien who smashed the English attack to smithereens with his barn-door like bat . It was the fastest hundred ( 50ball ) in a World Cup fixture and propelled Ireland to complete the highest successful run chase ( 327 ) in the tournament 's history. O'Brien entered the fray when the score read 106-4. When he left Ireland were 317 , within striking distance of a famous and England five-feet under . O'Brien smashed six sixes and 13 fours in his whirlwind stay at the crease. When it all ended , England were left holding their heads in despair .
