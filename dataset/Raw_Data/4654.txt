World Cup 2015 : India snub supporters at SCG The author has posted comments on this article IANS | Mar 25 , 2015 , 06.40PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 SYDNEY : India 's disregard for their fans continued on Wednesday with their supporters locked out of the Sydney Cricket Ground ( SCG ) and captain Mahendra Singh Dhoni refusing to fulfill his media obligations on the eve of their ICC Cricket World Cup 2015 semifinal match against Australia . Vice-captain Virat Kohli made some effort to acknowledge the people by signing a few autographs through the fence for the dozens shut out on Driver Avenue. The rest of his teammates simply retreated to the dressing rooms , reports news.com.au . A whopping 70 percent of Wednesday 's capacity crowd are estimated to be Indians . Asked why his skipper was absent from his obligatory pre-match press conference , his replacement Rohit Sharma was unable to provide a plausible explanation. Asked if there was anything wrong with Dhoni , Sharma replied , " He 's fine.
