World Cup 2015 : Misbah saved by the bail against Australia The author has posted comments on this article AFP | Mar 20 , 2015 , 10.45AM IST Josh Hazlewood of Australia reacts after nearly getting the wicket of Misbah-ul-Haq of Pakistan during the 2015 ICC Cricket World Cup quarterfinal match. ( Getty Images ) Page 1 of 4 ADELAIDE ( Australia ) : Pakistan captain Misbah-ul-Haq on Friday became the second player at the World Cup to be granted a reprieve when his stumps were hit without dislodging the bails . Scorecard Misbah was on nought , and his team in deep trouble at 24 for two in their quarterfinal against Australia at the Adelaide Oval , when Josh Hazlewood beat the 40-year-old for pace and clattered his leg stump , firing up the LED lights on the stumps . Full Coverage : ICC World Cup 2015 However , the bails remained in place and Misbah survived . It was an echo of Ed Joyce 's lucky break in Ireland 's match against the United Arab Emirates in Brisbane last month . UAE seamer Amjad Javed thought he 'd struck with his fourth ball when an inswinger beat left-hander Joyce 's defence and caused the LED lights on the bails to flash .
