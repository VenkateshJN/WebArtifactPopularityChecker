Darren Bravo reprimanded for using obscenity Sat , Feb 21 2015 Last updated on Saturday , 21 February , 2015 , 10 : 26 AM Bravo , who made 49 , retired due to hamstring injury. © Getty West Indies ' Darren Bravo has been reprimanded for blurting out an obscenity while batting against Pakistan in their Pool B ICC World Cup match in Christchurch on Saturday , an ICC press release said following the Caribbean team 's 150-run win . The West Indian was found guilty of breaching Level 1 Article 2.1.4 of the ICC Code of Conduct for Players and Player Support Personnel , which relates to " language or a gesture that is obscene , offensive or insulting during an International match " . All Level 1 breaches carry a minimum penalty of an official reprimand and a maximum penalty of 50 per cent of a player 's match fee . The left-hander , who scored 49 before a hamstring injury forced him to retire , pleaded guilty in front of match referee David Boon , after which the Australian ruled out any possibility of a formal hearing . Meanwhile , the West Indies Cricket Board came out with an update on Bravo 's injury. " Darren Bravo will stay with the team despite sustaining a left hamstring injury.
