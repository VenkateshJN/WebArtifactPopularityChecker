World Cup 2015 : The blues at the Big G The author has posted comments on this article Santosh Suri , TNN | Feb 23 , 2015 , 10.36AM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 The Indian tricolour fluttered proudly and the capacity Melbourne Cricket Ground ( MCG ) was painted in blue as Indian fans virtually took over the venue . In Adelaide , the Pakistani fans at least had some presence , but at the MCG , it was very difficult to seek out South African fans among the official attendance of 86,876. There were a few South African flags and one even spotted former SA skipper Graeme Smith singing the national anthem with the fans , but they were easily outnumbered. It was almost like a home game for India . ANOTHER REFERRAL GOES INDIA 'S WAY India have been wary of the DRS and have opposed it tooth and nail in bilateral tourneys. But in the World Cup , it has been made mandatory by the ICC. For the second game running , the men in blue have benefitted from the referral system. At the MCG too , the DRS went in India 's favour when Vernon Philander sought to review Kettleborough 's lbw verdict against R Ashwin. Coincidentally , the third umpire again was Davis as he said it was the umpire 's call after replays suggested that the ball would have clipped the top of middle stump . TENDULKAR STILL A CROWD FAVOURITE Indian legend Sachin Tendulkar was at the MCG to watch India decimate South Africa on Sunday. The moment he was shown on the giant screen , the crowd went berserk and started the famous S-A-C-HI-N , S-A-C-H-I-N chant. It brought back memories of the Little Master thumping the best of bowlers all over the world . KUMBLE GETS HIS CAP Anil Kumble was officially inducted into the ICC Hall of Fame during the change of innings when he received a special cap on the occasion .
