( AP Photo ) Page 1 of 4 MANCHESTER : England captain Alastair Cook looked relaxed and at peace with himself on Wednesday , the eve of the fourth Test against India . He had reasons to be pleased after knowing that his premier pacer James Anderson would be available for selection after he was given a clean chit by judicial commissioner Gordon Lewis for his spat with Ravindra Jadeja . " It has been a good couple of days in the sense that Jimmy is available to play and the whole incident is behind us and we can concentrate on playing cricket , " Cook said , vowing not to carry any hangover of the Trent Bridge incident into the Test match . Asked whether Anderson is in the right frame of mind to play the match , Cook said : " He 's a little bit less grumpy. It 's great that this is now behind him and he gets to play in a home Test match. To be playing here means a lot to him. " Cook disagreed with the suggestion that Anderson perhaps needs to keep his mouth shut after this incident . " Of course there are little instances where he might have overstepped the mark throughout his career but you 'd rather be on that line than too passive. He needs that for his bowling and the way he bowled , especially at Southampton , was incredible , " Cook opined .
