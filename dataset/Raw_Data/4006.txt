Mumbai move in for the kill , need 8 wickets The author has posted comments on this article Tushar Tere , TNN | Feb 1 , 2015 , 02.02PM IST Share on Linkedin Page 1 of 4 VADODARA : Mumbai are within striking distance of an outright victory against Baroda in the Group 'A ' Ranji Trophy match at the Motibaug Ground in Vadodara. Having entered the game as underdogs and in desperate need of a win , Mumbai batted and bowled well on a slow wicket prepared to give Baroda a home advantage. The visitors scored 304 runs for the loss of nine wickets in the second innings , setting a huge target of 407 runs for Baroda . With the wicket getting slower , the hosts face an uphill task on Sunday. With a decent lead of 103 runs in the bag , Mumbai openers Akhil Herwadkar and Shrideep Mangela gave a good start to the team on Saturday. But Yusuf Pathan broke the 45run partnership in 14th over when he clean bowled Herwadkar. Shrideep fell soon to a quick single as Saurabh Wakaskar took a perfect aim at the stumps to run out the opener. First innings centurion , Mumbai skipper Aditya Tare and Suryakumar Yadav too could n't add much to the scoreboard. Mumbai were down to 84 for four in 24th over . But Shreyas Iyer and Siddhesh Lad pulled Mumbai out of trouble by stitching a partnership of 114 for the fifth wicket. The hosts survived a scare when Kedar Devdhar was hit on the back of his head while fielding at short leg. Siddhesh pulled a delivery off Yusuf that struck Kedar at the bottom of his helmet. He complained of dizziness and was taken for a CT scan immediately. The reports were normal , said Baroda coach Tushar Arothe .
